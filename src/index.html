Alphonse Mucha

"What is it, Art Nouveau?...Art can never be new." -Alphonse Mucha

Mucha working on a poster for Cassan publishing house in 1896

Notable events in the life of Alphonse Mucha

  • 1860: Mucha is born on 24 July in Ivancice, Moravia (now Czech Republic).
  • 1872: At age 12, Mucha is given a choral scholarship and attends the Gymnazium Slovanske in Brno.
  • 1877: After being expelled from school due to poor academic performance, Mucha is inspired by a fresco in a local church and resolves to become a professional artist.
  • 1878: Mucha begins doing decorative and design work for local theaters and magazines.
  • 1882: Mucha receives his first commission from Count Karl Khuen-Belasi. The Count becomes a financial and moral support for Mucha.
  • 1885: Mucha attends the Munich Academy for the Arts.
  • 1887: Mucha moves to Paris to attend Academie Julian.
  • 1888: Mucha attends the Academie Colarossi.
  • 1889: To earn income after being forced to leave Academie Colarossi, Mucha begins work as a professional illustrator by securing commissions for French and Czech publishers.
  • 1892: The Paris Salon is held at the Palais des Champs Elysees and is Mucha's first exhibition.
  • 1894: On 26 December, Mucha accepts a last-minute commission for a poster Sarah Bernhardt's production of Gismonda. The poster becomse one of Mucha's most famous works and is so popular at the time the public takes to cutting them down at night to collect. This leads to Mucha signing a 6-year contract with Sarah Bernhardt to design posters, costumes, stage sets, and more.
  • 1897: Printing firm Imprimerie Champenois licenses Mucha's works to companies and publications in Europe and America, making him world-famous.
  • 1897: Mucha holds his first solo exhibition.
  • 1898: Mucha branches into jewelry design and begins a 3-year partnership with jeweller Georges Fouquet.
  • 1904: Mucha travels to America to begin his new career as a society portrait painter.
  • 1906: Mucha marries Maruska Chytilova and they return to the US to live in Chicago.
  • 1909: 15 March, Mucha's first child, daughter Jaroslava, is born.
  • 1909: Mucha begins work on his magnum opus, the Slav Epic.
  • 1910: The Mucha family returns to Europe to live in Prague.
  • 1915: 12 March, Mucha's son and future biographer, Jiri, is born.
  • 1918: Czechoslovakia's first series of postage stamps are issued and feature Mucha's designs.
  • 1919: 5 July, the Bank of Czechoslovakia puts into circulation the 100 crown banknote, designed by Mucha. Between 1919 and 1931, the 5, 10, 20, 50, 500, and 1000 crown banknotes are also designed by Mucha and put into circulation.
  • 1920: The Slav Epic paintings are exhibited at the Chicago Art Institute and attract 50,000 viewers in a single week.
  • 1926: Mucha completes the final three Slav Epic paintings. This brings the total number of large canvases that make up the series to 20. The largest canvas is over 6 x 8 meters.
  • 1934: Mucha is made an Officier de la Legion d'Honneur in France by the President.
  • 1939: In March, Mucha is among the first arrested by the German Gestapo because of his Masonic activities and role as a Slav nationalist. He is released after several days of questioning.
  • 1939: 14 July, Mucha dies at the age of 78 after a period of rapidly declining health.
